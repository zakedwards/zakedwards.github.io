              A mereological defense of type-identity physicalism from Kripke's modal objection

              We claim the necessary truth of the physicalist proposition P to be vindicated, via a strictly physicalist account of phenomenal experience, as any illusion of contingency may be explained as a conflation with the contingently true proposition P′′.

              Is personal identity a matter of psychological continuity?

              We aim in this paper to forward a neo-Lockean defense of psychological continuity – albeit in a manner perhaps dissimilar to ordinary understanding – as solely constitutive of (any reasonable conception of) personal identity.
